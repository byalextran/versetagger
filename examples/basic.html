What is VerseTagger?

VerseTagger automatically detects Bible references in your web pages and displays verse content in elegant modals. Simply hover over any scripture reference to see the verse text!

One of the most well-known verses is John 3:16 AMP, which speaks of God's love for the world. Another powerful passage is Romans 8:28, which reminds us that all things work together for good. The promise in Jeremiah 29:11 NLT gives us hope for the future.

The Psalms offer comfort and praise, like Psalm 23:1-6 which speaks of the Lord as our shepherd. Reading Matthew 5:1-12 gives us the Beatitudes. And the wisdom of Proverbs 3:5-6 ESV teaches us to trust in the Lord.

The New Testament epistles are also rich with teaching. Consider 1 Corinthians 13:4-8 on love, Ephesians 2:8-9 on grace, and Philippians 4:6-7 on peace. The book of James 1:2-4 encourages us through trials.

Supported Formats

VerseTagger recognizes various reference formats:

  • Simple references: Gen 1:1, John 3:16, Rev 21:1
  • Verse ranges: Romans 8:1-4, Matthew 5:3-10
  • Books with numbers: 1 John 4:8, 2 Timothy 3:16-17
  • Version suffixes: John 1:1 ESV, Psalm 119:105 NIV
  • Abbreviations: Matt 6:33, Gen 1:1, Ps 119:11

How It Works

VerseTagger is initialized with a simple configuration. Here's what we're using for this example:

const versetagger = new VerseTagger({ proxyUrl: 'https://your-proxy.workers.dev', trigger: 'hover', // Show modal on hover colorScheme: 'auto', // Auto-detect light/dark mode defaultVersion: 'NIV', // Default Bible version autoScan: true // Automatically scan on load });

That's all the JavaScript you need! VerseTagger automatically:

  • Scans your page for scripture references
  • Converts them to interactive elements
  • Fetches verse content from the API
  • Displays it in a beautiful, accessible modal
  • Adapts to your user's color scheme preference
